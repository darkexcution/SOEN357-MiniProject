Overview

This project explores the UX/UI design process for a mobile app helping individuals with chronic conditions manage medications, doctor appointments, and communicate with healthcare professionals.

User Research

Methods

  • Surveys: Conducted an online survey targeting individuals with chronic health conditions to gather data on medication habits, appointment management, technology usage, and feature preferences for a health management app.

Feedback Survey

Google Form used to collect user feedback on the Health Companion Super App design and functionality.

Survey Results

Image 1 of 9

Findings

  • Users sometimes forget medication doses or appointments due to their personal reasons
  • Ease of use and clear notifications are critical due to 1/3 of the users being over 45.
  • Communication with healthcare providers, medication usage tracking and appointment scheduling is highly valued.
  • Users prefer to be reminded when they are about to miss a medication dose or appointment.

Dropped designs:


The color scheme was green originally but decided to change blue to make it more calming and less associated with illness. The original app design was more complex and had a daily progress bar, which was removed to simplify the interface and focus on core functionalities. The appointment page was redesigned to be more straightforward, removing the small text and icons to enhance readability and usability, especially for older users. The landing page was simplified to focus on the app's main features and benefits, removing unnecessary elements that could overwhelm users. These changes were made to improve the overall user experience and ensure the app is accessible and easy to use for all target users.

Usability Testing

For the full report on usability testing, please refer to the full report. Below is a summary of the testing plan.
  • Goals: Identify issues with adding medications, set reminders for the medications, scheduling appointments, and messaging doctors.
  • Participants: 5–10 users who have a form of chronic disease.
  • Tasks: Add medication, mark as taken, schedule appointment, message doctor.
  • Feedback: Post-test interview with questions in person or online

Reflection

For the full reflection and analysis, please refer to the full report.

The UX process showed that simplicity and accessibility are essential, especially for older users. Balancing different user needs required removing clutter and focusing on clear, calming design choices like a blue color scheme. Overall, the project revealed that ease of use is more valuable than adding many features, which can overwhelm users.
